# Byggemenskap Service Offering

## The Problem We're Solving

Sweden's byggemenskap (community-led housing / CLH) sector is significantly behind the Netherlands, Denmark, and other EU countries. Two root causes:

1. **Awareness gap** — Most people don't know byggemenskap exists as an option for getting housing.
2. **Formation gap** — Even when there's will, professionals, and housing demand, the early stage of forming a group is the hardest. You need enough trust and familiarity to feel comfortable committing to building your housing together. That level of social capital takes time and a process to develop.

Platforms like [Crowdbuilding](https://crowdbuilding.com) (Netherlands) and [Almenr](https://almenr.dk) (Denmark) address the awareness and coordination layer — but the deeper challenge is the social formation layer that happens before anyone is ready for a platform.

## The Missing Piece: Group Formation

The real bottleneck is not knowledge, money, or land — it's that there is no repeatable process for forming new groups, building the social bonds needed to commit together, and carrying the knowledge and expertise between projects.

Once a byggemenskap group successfully builds their housing, they are simply glad to be done. They don't want to start another project. All their hard-won lessons and experience stay within that group. There is no actor systematically:
- Forming new groups
- Facilitating the social process of trust-building
- Accumulating and transferring institutional knowledge across projects

This is the strategic gap Parti Design is positioned to fill.

## Our Solution: Three-Layer Service

### Layer 1 — Awareness & Education
Provide knowledge and inspiration so that people understand byggemenskap as an option and feel they have a safe, reliable partner to develop their project. The website/page is the primary touchpoint for this.

**Targets:**
- Individuals who want to build together
- Municipal officials who want to support this type of development
- Funders and partners who want to contribute to the sector

### Layer 2 — Early Group Formation (the core innovation)

**Co-building as social infrastructure**

Hands-on collaborative building projects are a proven way to develop the social bonds required to step into a byggemenskap commitment together. The Umeå Kallbad sauna build demonstrated this clearly: volunteer time spent building, sharing meals, and solving problems together produced significant social capital — a sense of connection, empowerment, and trust that comes from achieving something together that none could have done alone.

**Open Collective via Massvis**

Through Parti Design's work with Massvis, a lightweight onboarding path has been developed: new groups can quickly register as a collective via Open Collective, start organizing and doing things together, and access fiscal hosting — without the overhead of forming a legal entity, doing paperwork, or handling accounting. This is the ideal first step for a loose-knit group that isn't ready to form an economic association.

**Grant support (Boverket)**

Boverket offers a grant of up to **400,000 SEK** for new byggemenskap groups (one application per organisation, ever). The grant is confirmed active with an annual appropriation of 4 million SEK. Legal basis: SFS 2019:676, most recently amended by SFS 2024:1035 (in force 31 December 2024).

**Eligibility criteria:**
- Minimum 6 members, all natural persons (not companies)
- Formed as an **ekonomisk förening** OR a **lokalt utvecklingsbolag** (a natural-persons-only LLC with profit restrictions — added in the December 2024 amendment)
- Each member/co-owner has contributed at least 10,000 SEK
- The group **owns the land or holds a binding contractual right to use it** (e.g. an option agreement or purchase agreement) — simply having identified a plot is not sufficient
- The municipality must permit residential construction on that land (no building permit required, but residential use in principle must be confirmed)

**What the grant covers:**
Pre-construction planning costs only ("inledande projektkostnader") — architect fees, project management, and other preparation costs incurred before construction begins. Costs incurred before the grant decision, and member labour/materials, are not eligible.

**Note on building type:** As of the December 2024 amendment, single-family and two-family homes now qualify — previously only multi-family buildings (flerbostadshus) were eligible.

Parti Design can support groups in applying for this grant. This funding is intended to cover the planning and design costs before construction begins, making it a critical bridge resource.

**What Parti Design offers in this layer:**
- Facilitate co-building projects that bring potential group members together
- Onboard new groups to Open Collective via Massvis for easy early-stage organizing
- Support groups in applying for grants (Boverket and others)
- Carry the process knowledge between projects — institutional memory across groups

### Layer 3 — Lead Development (Venture)

The next level is for Parti Design to actively form and lead a byggemenskap venture — not waiting for the right group to emerge organically, but initiating and jump-starting the process. This means:
- Recruiting group members
- Securing land
- Facilitating the full process
- Delivering a completed development

This transforms the service into a repeatable venture model that de-risks the sector and proves the concept. Each successful project creates visible evidence for municipalities, funders, and the public.

## Why This Is the Right Moment

- Swedish CLH sector is at an early stage — early movers have disproportionate influence
- Municipality interest in alternative housing models is growing (housing shortage pressure)
- The Boverket grant is underutilized — many groups don't know it exists or how to qualify
- Massvis / Open Collective infrastructure is already in place
- Parti Design has direct proof-of-concept from Umeå Kallbad (co-building → social capital)
- No current actor in Sweden is systematically doing early-stage group formation + knowledge transfer

| Segment | What they need | CTA |

| Individuals curious about building together | Inspiration + explanation of how it works + that it's achievable | Join a group / Register interest |

| Funders / foundations | Vision + systemic case + partnership framing | Get in touch |

## Page Goals (MVP)

1. Explain the opportunity and the gap clearly — build awareness
2. Make Parti Design the obvious partner for someone who wants to start
3. Create a simple onboarding path: learn → express interest → get supported → form group
4. Position toward municipalities and funders as a systemic actor, not just a consultancy
