# Target Audience

## Primary audience

**Municipalities and public institutions** (kommuner, regional bodies, public agencies)

**Who they are:**
Decision-makers at Kommuner and public agencies who are responsible for community development, placemaking, social inclusion, or sustainability initiatives. They have budgets and procurement processes. They tend to be innovation-minded but risk-averse — they want to work with someone creative and forward-thinking, but need to feel they are hiring a reliable, established, professional practice.

**What they're looking for:**
A credible partner who can help them engage their community, facilitate complex multi-stakeholder processes, and deliver tangible outputs (strategies, workshops, reports, sites, spaces). They may not fully know what they need yet — they respond well to a studio that comes with ideas and a clear point of view.

**What would make them reach out:**
- A clear portfolio of similar work (especially public/community projects)
- Evidence of competence and reliability alongside creativity
- An understandable description of what Parti Design does and how they work
- A sense that Parti Design will be a genuine collaborator, not just a vendor

## Secondary audiences

### Property developers

**Who they are:**
Local and regional developers managing residential or mixed-use properties — particularly those interested in community-oriented, sustainable, or innovative approaches (e.g. student housing, co-housing).

**What they need:**
Strategy and facilitation for engaging residents, as well as design and project management for place-based interventions. They need to trust the studio professionally. They may be skeptical of "community" framing but respond to outcomes and practical results.

---

### Community-based organisations & initiatives

**Who they are:**
NGOs, cooperatives, grassroots groups, housing associations, and local initiatives working on projects that involve their community members. Often working with smaller budgets but high engagement.

**What they need:**
A studio that genuinely believes in participatory process and can help them design and build something together with their members — architecture, co-building, co-design. They value alignment of values as much as credentials.

---

### Private individuals & small businesses

**Who they are:**
Clients seeking a website, branding, or digital service — or in some cases residential architecture. Often arriving through personal referral. May include international clients (e.g. NYC contacts) for digital/branding work.

**What they need:**
A clear understanding of what they'll get, a studio they trust creatively, and a professional but human relationship.

---

## Audience we are NOT designing for

- Clients looking for the cheapest or fastest solution with no care for quality or impact
- Clients who want to cause harm to people or planet for private gain and have no intention of changing
- Swedish-only speakers (for now — the practice operates entirely in English)

---

## What our audience already knows

Varies significantly by segment:
- **Municipalities:** Familiar with procurement, design briefs, project management. May not know terms like "regenerative" or "byggemenskap" — use plain language alongside specialist framing.
- **Community organisations:** Often highly engaged with participatory concepts but may not know architectural or digital terminology.
- **Private/small business clients:** Generally not familiar with design or architecture language — clarity and warmth matter more than credentials.

In all cases: **do not assume they understand the full breadth of what Parti Design offers.** The site needs to make that legible.

## Where our audience finds us

Currently, almost entirely through:
- Personal networking and relationships (Kasimir's primary sales channel)
- Pitching and proactive proposals
- Referrals from past clients and collaborators

The site exists partly to **support those conversations** (leave-behind, credibility piece) and partly to **start generating inbound discovery** for the first time.

Potential future discovery channels:
- Referrals from Coompanion, Byggemenskaps föreningen, and other partner networks
- Press or coverage of projects (Rewilding Sweden, DIT Egnahem, etc.)
- Search for byggemenskap, co-design, or placemaking services in Sweden
- International referrals for digital/branding work
