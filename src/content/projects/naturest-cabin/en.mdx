## The Idea

Naturest Cabin is a prototype for a new kind of small building in Sweden — off-grid, self-built, and high-performing. It uses the WikiHouse system: an open-source, CNC-cut timber structural frame that can be assembled by a small community team in a weekend, without specialist construction knowledge or heavy machinery.

The cabin was developed as a proof of concept for the DIT Egnahem venture — Parti Design's initiative to bring WikiHouse-based self-build housing to Sweden. The goal was to demonstrate that community self-build is not just viable but can produce architecture that is genuinely beautiful and thermally excellent.

## How It's Built

The structural frame is cut from standard sheet materials at a local CNC workshop. The parts slot together like a giant puzzle — each joint is designed so that the assembly sequence is intuitive and error-proof. No nails, no bolts, no expert labour. The entire frame was assembled by a small group over two days.

The building envelope was designed to passive-house-equivalent standards — a thick, continuous insulation layer with a smart breather membrane and modular prefabricated facade panels. The result is a structure that is both extremely airtight and able to handle moisture intelligently, suitable for the Swedish climate.

## Off-Grid Systems

The cabin runs entirely off-grid. Electrical supply comes from roof-mounted solar panels with battery storage. Water is handled through a rainwater collection and filtration system. Heating is provided by a small wood-burning stove, with the high-insulation envelope keeping heat loss minimal even in deep winter.

This makes Naturest Cabin suitable for remote sites that lack infrastructure — forest plots, island locations, and rural land without mains connections.

## A Platform for DIT Egnahem

Beyond the physical cabin, the project developed all the documentation and guidance needed to replicate the system: structural calculations adapted for Swedish building codes, a Swedish-language assembly guide, and a cost breakdown showing that a community self-build can achieve this quality at a fraction of conventional construction costs.

Naturest Cabin is the first WikiHouse to be built in Sweden. It is the foundation for DIT Egnahem's broader mission: making high-quality, architecturally considered housing accessible to communities who want to build it themselves.
