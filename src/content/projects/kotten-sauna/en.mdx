Kotten is the first sauna of Umeå Kallbad — built over one year through community-powered Sunday build days. Its name and form are drawn from the Swedish word for pinecone, and its facade is clad entirely in hand-cut shingles made from reclaimed pallet wood, each one shaped by the hands of over fifty volunteers.

It is a small building with a big story: proof that the way you build something can be just as meaningful as what you build.

## From Fika Wagon to Sauna

The journey began with an unlikely starting point — an old fika wagon used by construction workers on a building site. Umeå Kallbad had a vision and a growing community behind them, but no permanent location and no guarantee of a permit. What they did have was momentum: a grassroots crowdfunding campaign that raised 65,000 SEK from over 100 backers through nothing more than word of mouth and social media. That early surge of support made one thing unmistakably clear — there was real appetite for this.

## Co-Building as Community Strategy

The decision to build with volunteers wasn't simply a matter of budget — it was a deliberate strategy. Every Sunday for a year, a group of enthusiasts gathered at the site to work on the sauna, share a meal, and get to know each other. These weren't just build days; they were the founding moments of a community. The shared labour forged bonds and a collective sense of ownership that no marketing campaign could replicate.

Parti Design facilitated the design process in close collaboration with the core team — Sebastian Ulonska, Jon Krebs, and Garei Gattaulin — and with the volunteers themselves. The design emerged from dialogue, shaped over time by the same people who would build and use it.

## A Design Born from Nature

Kotten takes its name — and its silhouette — from the pinecone. The overlapping shingle cladding was drawn directly from this reference, each layer echoing the scales of the cone in miniature. The shingles were made entirely from reclaimed pallet wood: pallets were broken apart by hand, cut to size, and fixed one by one across the facade. It is the kind of labour-intensive detail that would be uneconomical in conventional construction, but is perfectly suited to volunteer effort — repetitive, accessible, and deeply satisfying to contribute to.

Natural wood fibre insulation completes the material palette, keeping the building warm, breathable, and rooted in its forest-adjacent setting at Bölesholmerna.

## One Winter to Prove It

With a single-season trial permit granted at Bölesholmerna, Kotten opened its doors for its first winter. Each Sunday, hundreds of people joined free community cold-bathing sessions — a ritual that quickly became a fixture of Umeå life. The season was an unqualified success. On the strength of it, the Kommun granted a permanent building permit, and Kotten became a lasting feature of the city's public landscape.

## The Story Is the Project

Umeå Kallbad understood early that the story of how a community space is made is just as powerful as the space itself. Kotten is evidence of that. The shingles carry the fingerprints of fifty volunteers. The timber holds the memory of a hundred Sunday mornings. And the warmth inside is not just from the stove — it is the accumulated energy of a community that built something together, and keeps coming back because of it.

That story now lives in the DNA of Umeå Kallbad. Kotten is its first, most tangible expression.
