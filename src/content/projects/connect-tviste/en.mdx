Connect Tviste was a prototype week project exploring how to create a greater sense of belonging at Tviste — a student housing area in Umeå run by Kajeka's Campus X. Through resident interviews and user journey mapping, we identified that students moved in to an area without community infrastructure: no welcoming contact, no indoor gathering spaces, and no shared ownership of the neighbourhood.

Working across service design, spatial thinking, and digital product, the team designed a connected system of three interventions — a repurposed community building (Tvistehuset), a paid resident advisor role (Husvärd), and a redesigned digital platform — all tied together through a resident-led student organisation called Tviste Club with its own mini-grant fund.

## The Problem

Students arriving at Tviste found themselves in a neighbourhood without community. No one welcomed them. There were no indoor places to gather. Resources like bikes and tools sat wasted. Spontaneous socialising only happened in summer, when the sun brought people outside. The result: low ownership, low safety, and a high churn of disconnected residents.

Key insights from our research pointed to three missing ingredients: *ownership*, *agency*, and a sense of *home*.

## Tvistehuset — A community club house

We proposed repurposing an existing building at the heart of Tviste into a community hub. The programme includes a social space, an info desk, toilets, and a tools and repair studio. Two adjacent bike sheds are converted into Tvistebanken — a shared resource storage managed by residents — with summer storage and smart object detection for security.

## Husvärd — Resident Advisors

Kajeka hires existing residents as paid community activators working part time. Husvärdar welcome new students, manage Tvistebanken, organise events, and coordinate student initiatives. They work in rotating groups across three responsibilities: Tvistebanken, Mini Grants, and Tvistehuset.

## Campus X Platform — A redesigned digital home

We redesigned the Campus X website to serve as the connective tissue between students and the neighbourhood. A new Services section partners with Umeå Energi, LF Västerbotten, Umeå Kommun, and Umeå Universitet to help students navigate moving in, home safety, internet setup, and student health. A dedicated Tviste Club section surfaces community events and the mini-grant programme.

## Tviste Club — Community with a budget

Tviste Club is a student organisation hosted on Open Collective through Massvis, giving residents a transparent, manageable budget for Tvistehusets. A mini-grants flow lets any resident apply for funding — applications reviewed and approved by the Husvärd, receipts paid out by Massvis. This gives students real financial agency to activate their own neighbourhood.

## Graphic Profile

As part of the prototype we also developed a graphic identity for the Tviste Area — earthy reds and forest greens, with Be Vietnam Pro for headings and Roboto for body text.
